Please correct the AI logic for cases where an order’s projected Estimated Completion Date is later than its Avail Date.

Example from the preview:
- Avail Date = May 12, 2026
- Estimated Completion Date = May 13, 2026 - 04:18 AM

In this case, the AI is still effectively using May 12 for the order even though the order is projected to complete on May 13. This should be treated as a delay-risk case and should not be considered a valid same-day suggestion.

IMPORTANT:
Estimated Completion should generally be used as a guide, not a universal hard blocker for all orders. However, when the comparison is:

Estimated Completion > Avail Date

that is a meaningful conflict and must influence the AI decision.

## Required behavior
If an order’s projected Estimated Completion exceeds its Avail Date:
- the AI should recognize the order as likely delayed
- the AI should try to advance it earlier if feasible
- the AI should try a better start anchor if feasible
- the AI should not continue presenting the same date as if it is still valid
- if no earlier feasible option exists, the AI should explicitly mark/explain the delay risk

## Clarification
This rule is specifically for conflict cases where:
- completion is later than avail date

This should not incorrectly affect cases where:
- completion is earlier than avail date

Example:
- Avail Date = May 22
- Estimated Completion = May 13
This is not a problem and should not trigger forced correction.

But this case:
- Avail Date = May 12
- Estimated Completion = May 13 04:18 AM
is a problem and must be handled.

## Sequence insight
When this happens, the sequence insight should explain it clearly, for example:

“This order is projected to complete after its avail date under the current sequence timing, so it should be advanced earlier or treated as delay-risk.”

## Verification
1. Orders with Estimated Completion later than Avail Date are treated as conflict cases
2. The AI no longer keeps the same date suggestion without acknowledging the delay
3. The AI attempts earlier placement or better anchoring where feasible
4. If not feasible, the order is clearly explained/flagged as at risk
