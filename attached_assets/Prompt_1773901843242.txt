## AUTO-SEQUENCE — INTEGRATE NEXT 10 DAYS TARGET DATES

Update the Auto-Sequence feature to incorporate the Next 10 Days stock level data when building the optimal sequence.

### UPDATED AI ANALYSIS FACTORS

The Auto-Sequence AI now considers the following when sequencing (in priority order):

**Priority 1 — Orders with actual Avail Dates:**
- Sorted chronologically (earliest first).
- Must complete on or before their Avail Date.
- This is unchanged from before.

**Priority 2 — Orders with inferred Target Dates (from Next 10 Days):**
- Non-dated orders (prio replenish, safety stocks) that have a calculated target date from the Next 10 Days stock level data.
- Sorted chronologically by target date (earliest first).
- INTERLEAVED with actual dated orders based on date.
- Must complete on or before their target date to avoid stockout.

**Priority 3 — Non-dated orders WITHOUT target dates:**
- Orders with non-date avail (prio replenish, safety stocks) that have NO match in the Next 10 Days data.
- No date constraint — placed in gaps or after all dated/targeted orders.
- Fill available production time.

**Priority 4 — Stock Sufficient orders:**
- Orders matched in Next 10 Days but flagged as "Stock Sufficient" (due for loading already >= inventory).
- Lowest priority — placed at the very bottom.
- Produced only if there is remaining capacity after all other orders.

---

### AUTO-SEQUENCE ALGORITHM UPDATE

```
Step 1: Collect all orders in the line.

Step 2: Categorize each order:
  - Category A: Has actual avail date → use avail date
  - Category B: Has inferred target date from Next 10 Days → use target date
  - Category C: Non-dated, no target date, no match → no date constraint
  - Category D: Stock Sufficient → lowest priority

Step 3: Sort Categories A and B together chronologically by date.
  - Treat actual avail dates and inferred target dates equally.
  - If an actual avail date and a target date fall on the same day,
    actual avail date takes priority (it's a hard deadline).

Step 4: Build the sequence:
  a. Place Category A + B orders in chronological order.
  b. Work backwards from each order's date to determine 
     when production must start.
  c. Fill gaps between dated/targeted orders with Category C 
     orders to maximize plant uptime.
  d. Place Category D orders at the very end.

Step 5: Verify and flag conflicts:
  - If any Category A order would miss its avail date → red conflict.
  - If any Category B order would miss its target date → amber warning.
  - If a Category C order placed in a gap causes a downstream 
    dated/targeted order to miss its deadline → yellow warning.
```
