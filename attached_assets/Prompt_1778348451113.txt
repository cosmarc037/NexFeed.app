Please improve the auto-sequence strategy cards so they show whether a proposed sequence will result in overdue, delayed, or otherwise compromised orders, using a simple summarized risk metric on the card.

---

## OBJECTIVE

Right now the strategy cards show:
- MTS adjusted
- Changeover time
- Time-saved

But they do not show an important operational consequence of the sequence:
- whether the proposed order arrangement causes any orders to become overdue, delayed, or otherwise compromised against future dispatch / target / stockout timing

This is a gap because a strategy may look acceptable on the card while still producing red overdue orders in the preview table.

We want the cards to surface this risk clearly, while keeping the card UI clean.

---

## REQUIRED IMPROVEMENT

Add a simple summarized risk indicator to each strategy card:

- `Orders at risk: X`

This should represent the total number of orders in that proposed sequence that become:
- overdue
- delayed
- compromised / at-risk

This keeps the card easy to scan.

---

## 1. USE A SINGLE SUMMARY METRIC ON THE CARD

On each strategy card, add one visible metric:

- `Orders at risk: X`

This should be shown alongside the other card metrics.

For example, cards would show:
- MTS adjusted
- Changeover time
- Time-saved
- Orders at risk

This should apply to:
- Standard Sequence
- AI sequence cards

So users can compare risk across all available strategies.

---

## 2. CALCULATE "ORDERS AT RISK" FROM THE ACTUAL RESULTING SEQUENCE

The value of `Orders at risk` must be based on the actual proposed resulting sequence for that strategy.

Use the final simulated sequence and determine how many orders become:
- overdue
- delayed
- compromised / at-risk

Do not use rough approximations.
It should be based on the actual preview result.

---

## 3. KEEP THE BREAKDOWN OUT OF THE CARD, BUT AVAILABLE ELSEWHERE

Do not show a multi-line breakdown on the card.

Instead:
- keep the card simple with `Orders at risk: X`

If needed, the breakdown can appear in:
- tooltip
- expanded AI reasoning
- debug/logging
- or another supporting detail area

For example, the reasoning may say:
- `Orders at risk: 3 (1 overdue, 1 delayed, 1 compromised)`

But the card itself should only show:
- `Orders at risk: 3`

---

## 4. ALIGN THE SUMMARY METRIC WITH TABLE HIGHLIGHTING

If the preview table shows orders as:
- overdue
- late
- compromised

then those same orders should contribute to the `Orders at risk` count on the card.

The card and the preview table must tell the same story.

If a strategy visibly creates red overdue rows, the card should reflect that through its risk count.

---

## 5. DEFINE WHAT COUNTS AS "AT RISK"

`Orders at risk` should include the total number of orders that fall into any of these categories under the proposed sequence:
- overdue
- delayed
- compromised / at-risk

Please use the app’s actual timing / target / future dispatch logic where available.

The goal is for one number to summarize all service/deadline risk caused by the sequence.

---

## 6. SHOW THE BREAKDOWN IN AI REASONING

In the expanded AI reasoning, include the more detailed breakdown.

Example:
- `Orders at risk: 3 (1 overdue, 1 delayed, 1 compromised)`

Or:
- `Orders at risk: 0`

This keeps the card simple while still letting users inspect the operational risk in more detail.

---

## 7. USE THIS METRIC IN RECOMMENDATION LOGIC TOO

This should not be display-only.

If a strategy causes more orders at risk, that should influence:
- recommendation
- auto-selection
- strategy ranking

For example:
- a strategy with lower changeover but higher `Orders at risk` may be worse overall than a safer strategy

So include this metric in the recommendation logic as an important operational factor.

---

## 8. KEEP THE CARD LAYOUT CLEAN

Add the new metric in a way that preserves readability.

Recommended visible card metrics:
- MTS adjusted
- Changeover time
- Time-saved
- Orders at risk

Do not clutter the card with extra explanatory text.

---

## 9. ADD DEBUG LOGGING FOR RISK SUMMARY

Temporarily log:

```javascript
console.debug('[Sequence Risk Summary]', {
  strategyId,
  title: strategy.title,
  ordersAtRisk,
  overdueCount,
  delayedCount,
  compromisedCount,
});
```

This helps verify:
- the summary count is correct
- the breakdown matches the displayed risk count
- recommendation logic sees the same result
