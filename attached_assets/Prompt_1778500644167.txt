Please simplify and correct the **Orders at risk** logic in the auto-sequence preview cards and AI reasoning so that it only counts orders that are truly overdue or delayed.

---

## OBJECTIVE

The current Orders at risk logic appears too aggressive and misleading.

Right now, the app may show results like:
- `3 | Low`
while the AI reasoning says:
- `Orders at risk: 3 (1 overdue, 2 compromised)`

This is confusing and does not align with how users interpret the dates shown in the preview table.

We want to simplify the logic so that Orders at risk only reflects orders that are actually:
- overdue
or
- delayed

Do not count future orders that are still within their allowed timing window.

---

## REQUIRED CHANGE

Replace the current risk classification logic with a simpler two-level model:

### High
If there are any orders already:
- past due
or
- delayed

### Low
If there are no orders already:
- past due
or
- delayed

Remove the “Moderate” state entirely.

Also stop using “compromised” as part of the card-level risk count if those orders are not actually late yet.

---

## 1. COUNT ONLY TRULY OVERDUE OR DELAYED ORDERS

The numeric count in `Orders at risk` must only include orders that are actually:
- overdue
- delayed
- already missed relative to the relevant timing requirement

Do **not** include orders that are merely:
- monitor
- upcoming
- due in the future
- near their threshold but not yet late
- “compromised” in an internal heuristic sense if they have not actually missed the date yet

This is the most important correction.

---

## 2. REMOVE “COMPROMISED” FROM THE CARD-LEVEL RISK MODEL

The current reasoning may say things like:
- `1 overdue, 2 compromised`

This should no longer drive the visible `Orders at risk` card count.

If those 2 “compromised” orders are still not actually overdue or delayed, they must not be counted in Orders at risk.

### Example
If:
- 1 order is overdue
- 2 orders are future-dated but monitored / at warning level

Then Orders at risk should show:
- `1 | High`

not:
- `3 | High`
and not:
- `3 | Low`

---

## 3. REMOVE “MODERATE” COMPLETELY

Do not use a Moderate risk level anymore.

Use only:

- `High`
- `Low`

### High
At least one overdue or delayed order exists

### Low
No overdue or delayed orders exist

This is the complete severity model.

---

## 4. UPDATE THE CARD DISPLAY FORMAT

Use the simplified inline display format:

- `2 | High`
- `3 | High`
- `0 | Low`
- `1 | High`

Do not use badge/pill UI for risk anymore.

Keep it simple.

### Color behavior
Keep font-color styling:
- `High` = red
- `Low` = green (or success/positive style)

If preferred for cleaner UI:
- keep the count neutral
- color only the severity word

Example:
- `1 | High` where only `High` is red
- `0 | Low` where only `Low` is green

## 5. ALIGN THE COUNT WITH THE ACTUAL TABLE DATES

The Orders at risk count must align with what the user can reasonably infer from the preview table.

If the visible relevant dates are:
- May 15
- May 16

and those are not yet overdue/delayed, then they must not be counted as at risk.

Only orders that are already missed / late should count.

This is critical for trust.

## 6. UPDATE AI REASONING TO MATCH THE SAME SIMPLIFIED LOGIC

The AI reasoning should no longer say:
- `Orders at risk: 3 (1 overdue, 2 compromised)`

if only 1 order is truly overdue/delayed.

Instead, it should reflect the same simplified logic.

### Example
- `Orders at risk: 1 overdue/delayed order`
or
- `Orders at risk: 1`
or
- `Orders at risk: 0`

The wording can vary, but the count must match the same rule used by the card.

Do not include non-late “compromised” orders in the visible reasoning count.
