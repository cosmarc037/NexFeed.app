## PRIMARY ISSUE TO CHECK

There may be hard-coded or stale logic in the application that keeps reusing an earlier date (such as May 12) even after:
- the order has been resequenced
- the order’s start has shifted
- the estimated completion is already on a later date
- or the category logic implies a different avail date basis

Please audit where Avail Date is currently sourced from and verify whether it is:
- stale
- cached incorrectly
- inherited incorrectly
- defaulted incorrectly
- or hard-coded in preview logic

---

## REQUIRED RULES FOR AVAIL DATE / AI DATE BASIS

Please enforce the following rules clearly:

### 1. CRITICAL ORDERS
If an order is **Critical** and there is **no target date from Future Dispatches** that the AI/app can use as the planning deadline, then:

## use TODAY’s date

This means:
- the app should not keep an old earlier date like May 12 if the current planning context is already May 13
- the AI must anchor critical no-target orders to the current day

This should be recomputed properly, not left stale.

---

### 2. URGENT AND MONITOR ORDERS
If an order is **Urgent** or **Monitor**, then the AI may advance it earlier, but:

## it must not go beyond the target date from Future Dispatches

Example:
- target date = May 20
- acceptable AI-suggested dates = May 13 to May 20
- unacceptable AI-suggested dates = May 21 onward

So these orders:
- can be advanced earlier
- but cannot be pushed later than their target date

Please ensure this is correctly enforced.

---

### 3. STOCK SUFFICIENT ORDERS
If an order is **Stock Sufficient**, then it is flexible.

The AI may:
- advance it earlier
- keep it near target
- or produce it later

with a flexibility window of:

## up to 14 days

This flexibility should be used for:
- load balancing
- changeover reduction
- profitability strategies
- utilization improvement

Please ensure this category is not treated as tightly as urgent/monitor.

---

## CONSISTENCY CHECK

The app should ensure Avail Date logic is internally consistent with:
- order urgency category
- future dispatch target date, if available
- today’s date, if no target exists for critical orders
- AI-suggested date strategy
- current preview timing context

The Avail Date shown should not remain stuck on an old date just because that was previously inferred earlier in the flow.

---

## IMPORTANT CLARIFICATION

Do **not** force Avail Date to equal Start Date or Estimated Completion Date.

Avail Date remains the requirement / planning deadline field.

However, the app must still ensure that the Avail Date shown is the correct one based on:
- urgency bucket
- Future Dispatch target logic
- critical fallback logic
- stock-sufficient flexibility rules

Right now, the problem is not that Avail Date differs from Start/Completion.

The problem is that the Avail Date itself appears wrong or stale.
