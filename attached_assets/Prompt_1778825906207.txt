Please add a rule in the auto-sequence feature so that Powermix-generated orders are not considered for cross-line movement.

These generated orders should remain fixed to the destination line assigned by the Powermix rule.

---

## REQUIRED RULE

If an order was auto-generated from Line 5 / Powermix Split, then it must **not** be included in the pool of orders that the AI may move to other lines during auto-sequencing.

### Meaning
- if a generated order is assigned to Line 2, it must stay in Line 2
- if a generated order is assigned to Line 6, it must stay in Line 6

Do not allow the auto-sequence logic to move these generated orders to a different line, even if the queue time is low or another line looks feasible.

---

## IMPORTANT DISTINCTION

These generated orders should still be included in sequencing logic **within their assigned line**.

So:
- they can still participate in AI sequencing
- they can still be re-sorted within the same line
- they can still be considered in sequence strategy analysis
- they can still affect changeover and queue sequencing

But:
- they must not be transferred to another line
- they must not be considered for cross-line reassignment

---

## ALIGNMENT WITH EXISTING LINE 5 RULE

This should follow the same concept as the current Line 5 source-order restriction:
- Line 5 source orders should not be moved to other lines
- generated Powermix orders should also not be moved away from their assigned destination line

So both source and generated Powermix-related orders are line-locked.

---

## SCOPE

Apply this rule specifically to:
- orders auto-generated from Line 5 / Powermix Split
- identified using the existing generated-order linkage / metadata

Do not apply this restriction to unrelated regular orders.

---

## AI BEHAVIOR EXPECTATION

During auto-sequence:
- generated orders may still be included in the strategy output
- the AI may still suggest how to sort them relative to other orders in the same line
- but the AI should not suggest moving them out of their assigned line

This means they are:
- sequenceable
- but not movable across lines
