Please refine the diversion behavior for combined orders so they are treated as a single group during diversion.

Currently, the app is still allowing sub-orders to behave too independently, which causes incorrect diversion behavior.

Please implement the following rules.

---

## 1. WHEN A COMBINED ORDER IS DIVERTED, ITS SUB-ORDERS MUST ALSO MOVE

If a combined order is diverted, the diversion must include:
- the lead order
- all of its linked sub-orders

The app must not divert only the lead order while leaving the sub-orders behind.

### Required behavior
When a lead order is diverted:
- move the entire combined group together
- preserve their group relationship in the destination line

The combined order should behave as one diversion unit.

---

## 2. SUB-ORDERS SHOULD NOT BE DIVERTED INDEPENDENTLY

When orders are combined, the sub-orders must not be independently divertible.

### Required behavior
- sub-orders should not be selectable as standalone diversion targets
- sub-orders should not show diversion behavior independent from the lead order
- only the **lead order** should act as the diversion control point

If a user attempts to divert a sub-order directly, the app should block it or ignore it appropriately.

### Correct behavior
- only the lead order can be diverted
- when the lead is diverted, all sub-orders move with it

This means combined orders are treated as a whole.

---

## 3. PRESERVE LEAD VS SUB VISUAL DISTINCTION FOR DIVERTABLE COMBINED ORDERS

Currently, combined orders already use blue highlighting where:
- lead order is a darker shade
- sub-orders are a lighter shade

Please extend the same visual principle to the diversion-eligible state.

### For combined orders that are divertable (e.g. due to shutdown)
If the group is highlighted in yellow because it can be diverted:
- the **lead order** should use a darker yellow highlight
- the **sub-orders** should use a lighter yellow highlight

This should help users distinguish:
- the lead/controller row
- from the supporting sub-order rows

---

## 4. PRESERVE LEAD VS SUB VISUAL DISTINCTION FOR ALREADY DIVERTED COMBINED ORDERS

Similarly, when the combined order has already been diverted and is highlighted in green:

### Required behavior
- the **lead order** should use a darker green highlight
- the **sub-orders** should use a lighter green highlight

This should mirror the same lead/sub pattern used in the blue combined-order state and the yellow divertable state.

---

## 5. COMBINED ORDER DIVERSION SHOULD BE GROUP-BASED

Please ensure the diversion logic treats the combined order group as one entity for:
- diversion eligibility
- diversion action
- moved destination line
- visual state/highlighting

### Important
Do not evaluate or move sub-orders as if they were unrelated standalone rows once they belong to a combined order group.

---

## 6. UI / INTERACTION EXPECTATION

### Lead order
- may show diversion affordance / right-click diversion logic if applicable
- acts as the group controller

### Sub-orders
- should not independently offer diversion
- should visually follow the lead’s diversion state
- should move together with the lead

---

## 7. PRESERVE GROUP RELATIONSHIP AFTER DIVERSION

When the combined order group is diverted:
- keep the lead/sub relationship intact in the destination line
- do not flatten or separate the group
- do not leave sub-orders in the original line

The diverted result should still visually and logically behave as one combined group.
