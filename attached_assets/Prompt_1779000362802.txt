Please fix the auto-sequence behavior for Powermix-generated orders.

There is currently a bug where a generated order is being moved to another line during auto-sequence, including being moved back into Line 5 and even being combined there. This is incorrect and contradicts the Powermix split rules.

---

## CONFIRMED ISSUE

Powermix-generated orders are supposed to stay in the destination line assigned by the Powermix rule.

However, the app is still allowing a generated order to be:
- moved to another line during auto-sequence
- including being moved into Line 5
- and then combined there

This should not happen.

---

## REQUIRED RULE

Powermix-generated orders must be **line-locked**.

### Meaning
If a generated order is created in:
- Line 2 → it must stay in Line 2
- Line 6 → it must stay in Line 6

It must not be moved by auto-sequence to:
- another destination line
- Line 5
- or any other line

---

## IMPORTANT

Generated orders should still be included in sequencing logic **within their assigned line**.

So they can still be:
- re-ordered within the same line
- included in in-line sequencing strategy
- included in changeover and queue analysis for that line

But they must not be included in:
- cross-line movement
- cross-line reassignment
- movement back to Line 5
- cross-line combine behavior that breaks the original Powermix split logic

---

## ALSO EXCLUDE GENERATED ORDERS FROM CROSS-LINE COMBINE SIDE EFFECTS

If the current auto-sequence / combine logic is causing generated orders to be moved and then combined in another line, this must be blocked.

Generated Powermix orders should not become free-moving combine candidates across lines.

### Required behavior
- do not move generated orders out of their assigned destination line
- do not move generated orders into Line 5
- do not allow auto-sequence to combine them in a way that contradicts the Powermix source/generated relationship

---

## POWERMIX RULE MUST TAKE PRIORITY

The Powermix split assignment is a hard line-assignment rule for generated orders.

Auto-sequence must respect this.

Even if:
- another line has low queue time
- another line seems compatible
- another line has a combine opportunity

the generated order must remain fixed to the line assigned by Powermix Split.

---

## WHAT TO CHECK

Please audit the auto-sequence flow for any logic that still incorrectly includes Powermix-generated orders in:
1. movable-order candidate pools
2. cross-line reassignment pools
3. combine candidate pools across lines
4. line-optimization logic that does not respect line lock metadata

Likely issue:
the generated order is still being treated as a normal movable order instead of a line-locked generated order.
