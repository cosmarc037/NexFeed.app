Please update the **Plant-Level Auto-Sequence** handling for **shutdown lines** so that shutdown lines are excluded as **destination lines**, but eligible orders on those shutdown lines can still be moved out to **active lines**.

---

## 1. REFINE THE SHUTDOWN RULE IN AUTO-SEQUENCE

The current shutdown behavior in auto-sequence appears too restrictive.

Right now, when a line is in shutdown, the app may treat that line as completely excluded from the auto-sequence process, including leaving its orders unmoved.

Please refine this behavior.

### New intended rule
If a line is in shutdown:

- it must **not** be used as a valid **destination line**
- but its eligible orders should still be considered as **source orders** that can be moved to other active lines

### Expected result
Orders on shutdown lines should not be stranded unnecessarily.
The optimizer should still try to move eligible shutdown-line orders to active lines where production can continue.

---

## 2. SHUTDOWN LINES MUST NOT RECEIVE ORDERS DURING AUTO-SEQUENCE

Please keep this rule:

If a line is in shutdown, auto-sequence must **not** place orders onto that line.

### This means shutdown lines are excluded from:
- cross-line movement destination evaluation
- destination scoring
- auto-sequence placement targets
- cross-line combination placement
- final active scheduling destinations

### Expected result
No order should be newly assigned onto a shutdown line while shutdown is active.

---

## 3. ELIGIBLE ORDERS CURRENTLY ON A SHUTDOWN LINE SHOULD STILL BE EVALUATED FOR MOVEMENT TO ACTIVE LINES

Please allow auto-sequence to consider orders currently on a shutdown line as **movable source orders**, provided they are otherwise eligible for movement.

### Goal
If the line is shutdown, the app should try to recover production by moving eligible orders off that line and onto active lines.

### Expected result
Orders on a shutdown line can still be evaluated and moved out during plant-level auto-sequence, as long as:
- they are eligible for movement
- a valid active destination line exists
- run-rate and other applicable rules are satisfied

---

## 4. APPLY NORMAL MOVEMENT ELIGIBILITY RULES TO SHUTDOWN-LINE ORDERS

Please preserve existing movement rules when evaluating orders on a shutdown line.

### This means shutdown-line orders can only move out if they still pass the normal applicable rules, such as:
- historical run-rate eligibility on the destination line
- not blocked by line-lock rules
- not blocked by source/generation restrictions
- not blocked by overlap rules
- not blocked by combine restrictions
- status eligibility rules still apply

### Important
This request is not asking to move every shutdown-line order blindly.

It is asking to allow **eligible shutdown-line orders** to participate as source candidates in auto-sequence.

### Expected result
Shutdown-line orders are movable only when they satisfy the same real business constraints as other cross-line moves.

---

## 5. PRESERVE STATUS-BASED PROTECTION FOR NON-MOVABLE ORDERS ON SHUTDOWN LINES

Please preserve the existing business rule that certain orders on a shutdown line should **not** be moved, such as orders already:
- `in_production`
- `on-going`
- `completed`
- or otherwise non-divertible / non-movable by current rules

### Expected result
Only valid movable orders on the shutdown line should be considered for auto-sequence movement.
Protected/non-movable orders should remain excluded.

---

## 6. DO NOT TREAT A SHUTDOWN LINE AS FULLY INVISIBLE TO THE OPTIMIZER

Please do not completely remove shutdown lines from consideration in a way that prevents their current eligible orders from being evaluated.

### Correct behavior
A shutdown line should be treated as:
- **not a valid destination**
- but still a **valid source of eligible movable orders**

### Expected result
The optimizer can still relieve a shutdown line by moving its eligible orders to active lines.

---

## 7. EXCLUDE SHUTDOWN LINES FROM CROSS-LINE COMBINATION DESTINATION LOGIC, BUT STILL ALLOW THEIR ELIGIBLE ORDERS TO MOVE OUT

Please keep shutdown lines out of:
- combine destination placement
- active destination selection
- final target-line assignment

But if an order currently sits on a shutdown line and is otherwise eligible, the optimizer should still be allowed to:
- move it out
- combine it on an active line if valid under existing combine rules
- place it into the active-line sequence

### Expected result
Shutdown lines should not receive combinations, but eligible orders on them can still be relocated and combined elsewhere when valid.

---

## 8. UPDATE THE PREVIEW UI MESSAGE SO IT DOES NOT IMPLY THAT NO ORDERS ON THE SHUTDOWN LINE WERE CONSIDERED

The current preview message may say something like:
- `Orders shown remain on this line and were not moved, combined, or rebalanced.`

That message is too absolute if the intended behavior is to allow eligible shutdown-line orders to move out.

### Please update the message
The UI should explain that:

- the shutdown line was excluded as a destination
- but eligible orders on that line may still be considered for movement to active lines
- only orders that could not be moved under the existing rules remain there

### Example wording
- `Line 1 is in shutdown and was excluded as a destination line in auto-sequence. Eligible orders on this line were evaluated for movement to active lines where possible.`
- `This line is in shutdown, so no orders were placed onto it. Eligible orders were considered for relocation to active lines based on normal movement rules.`

### Expected result
The preview messaging should accurately reflect the refined behavior.

---

## 9. KEEP THE SHUTDOWN INDICATOR IN THE PREVIEW UI

Please preserve the visual shutdown indicator in the preview, such as:
- a red shutdown badge
- muted/low-opacity styling
- a shutdown explanation banner/message

### Goal
Users should still clearly understand that:
- this line is in shutdown
- it was not used as a destination
- but its eligible orders may still have been evaluated for movement outward

---

## 10. APPLY THIS TO PLANT-LEVEL AUTO-SEQUENCE CROSS-LINE MOVEMENT LOGIC

Please ensure this refined shutdown behavior applies to the plant-level auto-sequence flow where the app:
- rebalances queue load
- moves orders across lines
- combines across lines
- generates AI sequencing proposals

### Expected result
The optimizer should behave consistently:
- shutdown lines do not receive orders
- active lines remain destinations
- shutdown-line eligible orders can still move out to active lines

---

## IMPORTANT

### Shutdown line as destination:

- do not place orders onto shutdown lines
- do not use shutdown lines as active combination destinations

### Shutdown line as source:
- **Allowed for eligible orders**
- eligible shutdown-line orders should still be considered for movement to active lines
