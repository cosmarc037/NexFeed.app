Please improve the **Auto-Sequence AI**, specifically the **MTS advancement logic**, so that when **MTS advancement is identified as a primary sequencing lever**, the AI more actively uses **underutilized earlier days** instead of leaving visible gaps in the schedule.

---

## 1. MAKE MTS ADVANCEMENT MORE AGGRESSIVE WHEN IT IS A PRIMARY LEVER

Right now, the AI can identify **MTS advancement** as:
- `HIGH`
- `PRIMARY`

but the resulting schedule can still leave obvious underutilized earlier days.

### Required improvement
When MTS advancement is identified as a primary strategy, the AI should more actively search for flexible orders that can be pulled earlier.

### Goal
Make the resulting sequence reflect the strategy that the AI itself says it is prioritizing.

### Expected result
If MTS advancement is primary, the final schedule should visibly show stronger early advancement of flexible orders.

---

## 2. MAKE THE AI LOOK FOR UNDERUTILIZED EARLIER DAYS AND FILL THEM

Please make the AI more intentional about identifying underutilized earlier dates and using them for safe MTS advancement.

### Examples of what the AI should consider
- Is **today** underutilized?
- Is **tomorrow** underutilized?
- Are intermediate dates like `June 6`, `June 7`, `June 8` lightly loaded or empty?
- Are there flexible orders currently sitting on later dates such as `June 9` or `June 14` that could safely be moved earlier?

### Goal
Do not leave earlier days obviously underloaded if there are flexible orders that can safely fill them.

### Expected result
The AI should make better use of visible schedule gaps.

---

## 3. IF TODAY OR AN EARLIER DATE IS LIGHTLY LOADED, LET THE AI PULL FLEXIBLE ORDERS FORWARD

If today (or another earlier day) has very little scheduled work, and there are flexible orders on later dates, the AI should consider moving some of that work earlier.

### Example
If:
- today is `June 4`
- `June 4` only has one short order
- and there are flexible orders sitting on `June 9` or `June 14`

then the AI should consider advancing some of those later orders into `June 4`, `June 5`, `June 6`, etc., where feasible.

### Goal
Use earlier days more efficiently.

### Expected result
The AI should not leave clearly underutilized earlier days unused while flexible work remains on much later dates.

---

## 4. DO NOT SKIP OBVIOUS OPEN DAYS WITHOUT GOOD REASON

There are cases where the AI suggests an order for `June 9`, even though:
- the last earlier order is on `June 5`
- and `June 6`, `June 7`, and `June 8` appear open or lightly loaded

### Required improvement
If the AI leaves those earlier dates unused, it should only do so for a strong reason.

### Goal
Prevent situations where the schedule visibly looks like it missed an obvious advancement opportunity.

### Expected result
The AI should either:
- use those earlier underloaded dates
or
- have a clear reason for not using them

---

## 5. MAKE MTS ADVANCEMENT REFLECT TRUE LOAD-BALANCING, NOT JUST MINOR EARLY SHIFTS

Please improve the AI so MTS advancement is not just:
- a few isolated earlier moves

but instead behaves like:
- meaningful workload spreading across earlier underutilized days

### Goal
If MTS advancement is marked primary, the final schedule should feel like a real load-balancing strategy.

### Expected result
The AI should spread flexible work more intelligently across earlier available dates.

---

## 6. KEEP THE AI BALANCING ADVANCEMENT WITH CHANGEOVERS AND OTHER FACTORS

The AI should still consider:
- changeover cost
- material grouping
- urgency / future dispatches
- effective/suggested dates
- production flow

but when MTS advancement is primary, it should not let these other factors completely prevent reasonable use of empty earlier days.

### Goal
Create better balance between:
- sequence efficiency
- and earlier workload utilization

### Expected result
The AI should still optimize changeovers, but not at the expense of leaving obviously underutilized earlier days unused.

---

## 7. MAKE THE FINAL SEQUENCE INSIGHT EXPLAIN WHY A LATER DATE WAS USED IF EARLIER DAYS WERE OPEN

If the AI intentionally keeps an order on a later date even though earlier days appear open, please make the Sequence Insight explain why.

### Goal
Help users understand why the AI did not use an earlier visible gap.

### Expected result
When an order stays on `June 9` or `June 14` despite open earlier days, the explanation should make that choice understandable.

---

## IMPORTANT

Please improve the AI’s **MTS advancement logic** so that when MTS advancement is identified as a **primary sequencing lever**, the AI more actively uses **underutilized earlier days**.

This means:
- detecting earlier lightly loaded days
- pulling flexible MTS orders forward more intentionally
- not skipping obvious open dates like `June 6`, `June 7`, or `June 8` without good reason
- making the final schedule visibly reflect the advancement strategy the AI says it is prioritizing

The goal is for the schedule to better utilize early capacity instead of leaving obvious gaps while flexible work remains on much later dates.
