Please improve the **Auto-Sequence AI** so that it better utilizes **open earlier dates** and does not leave obvious schedule gaps when there are later flexible orders that could be safely advanced.

---

## 1. MAKE THE AI MORE AGGRESSIVE ABOUT USING OPEN EARLIER DATES

There are still cases where the AI leaves visible earlier dates unused, even though there are later flexible orders that appear eligible for advancement.

### Current issue
The sequence sometimes shows patterns like:
- orders on `June 5`, then the next flexible orders start on `June 9`
- orders on `June 8`, then the next flexible orders jump to `June 14`
- today is around `June 4`, but the next suggested production dates still start much later

### Goal
The AI should use open earlier dates more aggressively when safe.

### Expected result
The AI should no longer leave obvious earlier gaps if there are later flexible orders that can be brought forward.

---

## 2. DO NOT SKIP OPEN DAYS WITHOUT A STRONG REASON

If there are open or lightly loaded days between two suggested date groups, the AI should not skip those days unless there is a strong operational reason.

### Examples of the issue
- `June 5` is used, but `June 6`, `June 7`, `June 8` are left unused, then work resumes on `June 9`
- `June 8` is used, but later flexible orders remain on `June 14`
- today is underutilized but the AI still starts flexible work several days later

### Goal
Make skipped earlier dates rare and intentional, not routine.

### Expected result
If an earlier date is skipped, it should be because the AI truly determined it should not be used — not because it overlooked the opportunity.

---

## 3. HAVE THE AI ACTIVELY SEARCH FOR GAP-FILLING OPPORTUNITIES ACROSS EARLIER DAYS

Please make the AI more actively look for open days between:
- the earliest scheduled work
- and later flexible orders

### The AI should consider questions like:
- Is there open capacity on `today` or `tomorrow`?
- Are there empty days between the current scheduled blocks?
- Are there later MTS/flexible orders that could safely move into those empty dates?
- Would moving them earlier improve utilization without violating business rules?

### Goal
Make gap-filling behavior more deliberate and more effective.

### Expected result
The AI should identify and use earlier gap-filling opportunities more often.

---

## 4. MAKE THE AI BETTER USE THE SAFE ADVANCEMENT WINDOW FOR FLEXIBLE ORDERS

For flexible / MTS / Sufficient orders, the AI should make better use of the allowable advancement window.

### Current concern
Even when the order appears flexible enough to move earlier, the AI still leaves it on a much later date while earlier dates remain empty.

### Goal
Use the available flexibility more effectively.

### Expected result
Flexible orders should more often move into earlier underutilized dates when it makes operational sense.

---

## 5. MAKE THE AI BALANCE GROUPING / CHANGEOVER WITH REAL DATE UTILIZATION

The AI should still consider:
- grouping
- changeover reduction
- material clustering
- flow optimization

but these should not cause the AI to ignore obvious open earlier dates without good reason.

### Goal
Do not let grouping logic dominate so strongly that the schedule ends up visibly underutilized across earlier days.

### Expected result
The final schedule should feel both:
- operationally grouped
- and date-utilization aware

---

## 6. IF THE AI LEAVES A GAP, MAKE THE REASON CLEAR

If the AI intentionally keeps later flexible work on a later date even though earlier dates appear open, the explanation should make that clear.

### Example of what the AI should explain
- why `June 6–8` were not used
- why a flexible order stayed on `June 9` or `June 14`
- why the earlier date was not the better placement despite appearing open

### Goal
Make skipped earlier dates understandable to the user.

### Expected result
When gaps remain, users can see a clear reason rather than assuming the AI missed the opportunity.

---

## 7. KEEP THIS IMPROVEMENT ESPECIALLY STRONG FOR MTS / FLEXIBLE ORDERS

This improvement should especially apply to:
- MTS orders
- Sufficient orders
- other flexible/advanceable orders

### Goal
These are the orders that should help the AI fill open earlier days.

### Expected result
The AI should use these orders more effectively to smooth utilization and reduce unnecessary gaps.

---
