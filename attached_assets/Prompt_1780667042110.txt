Please revisit the **AI-powered sequencing strategy generation**, specifically the relationship between the **THREE-DIMENSION ANALYSIS** and the **actual final proposed sequence**, because they currently appear too easy to drift apart.

The current behavior suggests that the AI can:
- describe one strategic emphasis in the 3D analysis
- but generate a final sequence that does not materially reflect that emphasis
- or produce measured results that do not support the claimed priority

This creates a disconnect where the analysis feels like a theoretical explanation, while the actual sequence and metrics tell a different story.

---

## 1. ENSURE THE 3D ANALYSIS IS DERIVED FROM THE FINAL SEQUENCE, NOT JUST INITIAL INTENT

Please review whether the AI is currently treating the **3D analysis** as an early conceptual framing instead of a description of what the final sequence actually does.

### Current concern
The AI may:
- identify a theoretical opportunity
- declare it as PRIMARY in the 3D analysis
- then produce a final sequence where that priority is weakly reflected or not realized at all

For example:
- the AI says **changeover reduction is primary**
- but the final sequence shows little or no meaningful regrouping
- and the measured result shows **0.00 hr time-saved**

### Goal
Make the 3D analysis reflect the **actual verified behavior of the final sequence**, not just the AI’s initial planning idea.

### Expected result
If the final sequence does not materially execute a claimed priority, that dimension should not be presented as PRIMARY in the final 3D analysis.

---

## 2. ADD A VERIFICATION STEP BETWEEN STRATEGY INTENT AND FINAL 3D LABELING

Please add or strengthen a verification step after the AI generates the final sequence.

### Current concern
The AI may be:
- naming a primary strategic emphasis too early
- before the final sequence has been fully constructed and evaluated

This makes it possible for the final explanation to overstate what the sequence actually achieved.

### Goal
Require the AI to:
1. identify candidate strategic opportunities
2. generate the feasible sequence
3. compare the final sequence to baseline / measured outcome
4. only then assign the final 3D labels and explanation

### Expected result
The final 3D analysis should be the **post-verification explanation** of the sequence, not a pre-committed narrative that survives even when the final outcome doesn’t support it.

---

## 3. DO NOT LET THE AI CLAIM A PRIMARY DIMENSION WITHOUT ACTUAL SEQUENCE EVIDENCE

Please tighten the prompt so the AI cannot label a dimension as PRIMARY unless the final sequence clearly contains concrete decisions in support of that dimension.

### Current concern
The AI can currently say things like:
- changeover reduction is primary
- MTS advancement is primary
- profitability is primary

even when the actual order moves or date placements do not strongly demonstrate that.

### Goal
Require direct sequence evidence for each claimed primary emphasis.

### Expected result
Examples:
- **Changeover reduction** should only be PRIMARY if the final sequence materially groups compatible orders or avoids costly transitions
- **MTS advancement** should only be PRIMARY if the final sequence materially advances eligible orders earlier
- **Profitability** should only be PRIMARY if the final sequence materially favors higher-margin feasible options

If those actions are not clearly present in the final sequence, the dimension should not be labeled as PRIMARY.

---

## 4. DO NOT PRESENT UNREALIZED OPPORTUNITIES AS IF THEY WERE ACHIEVED STRATEGIES

Please make the AI explicitly distinguish between:
- identified opportunity
- attempted action
- realized outcome

### Current concern
The AI may correctly notice that a line has a strong theoretical changeover opportunity, but if constraints prevent the final sequence from realizing that opportunity, the 3D analysis can still read as if the strategy actually achieved it.

That is misleading.

### Goal
Make the AI speak plainly when a strategy theme was identified but not actually realized.

### Expected result
If the AI identifies a promising changeover opportunity but the final verified result is unchanged, it should say something like:
- opportunity identified but not realized
- attempted clustering, but no net savings achieved
- constrained by date / line / monitor limits

It should **not** present that dimension as the defining successful priority of the strategy.

---

## 5. MAKE THE TITLE, SUMMARY, 3D ANALYSIS, AND METRICS ALL TELL THE SAME STORY

Please review the full strategy card output so that all components align:
- title
- summary
- key details
- what this line shows
- why this strategy fits this line
- 3D analysis
- actual result / verification note

### Current concern
Different parts of the card may currently describe different realities:
- title suggests one strategic theme
- 3D analysis suggests another
- metrics show little or no result
- actual-result block contradicts the narrative

### Goal
Make the strategy card feel internally consistent.

### Expected result
Users should not have to reconcile conflicting signals across different parts of the same strategy card.

If the actual result is neutral, the title and explanation should not sound like a strong optimization success.

---

## 6. MAKE THE FINAL 3D LABELS OUTCOME-BASED, NOT JUST OPPORTUNITY-BASED

Please review whether the AI is currently choosing PRIMARY / SECONDARY / DEPRIORITIZED labels based too much on **theoretical line opportunities** instead of **actual final-sequence behavior**.

### Current concern
A dimension may be labeled HIGH / PRIMARY simply because it looked like the strongest lever in principle, even if the final feasible sequence did not actually realize it.

### Goal
Make final 3D labels reflect what the final sequence actually did.

### Expected result
The final labels should be assigned only after checking:
- actual order re-sequencing
- actual suggested dates
- actual measured deltas versus baseline
- actual risk introduced or avoided
- actual realized improvements

---

## 7. PRESERVE THE AI’S ABILITY TO IDENTIFY OPPORTUNITIES, BUT DO NOT CONFUSE OPPORTUNITY WITH OUTCOME

This is not a request to remove strategic diagnosis.

### Important
The AI should still be allowed to identify:
- changeover opportunities
- advancement opportunities
- profitability opportunities
- tradeoffs across the line

### But
Those opportunities should not automatically become the final 3D story unless the resulting sequence actually reflects them.

### Goal
Preserve good strategic analysis while ensuring the final user-facing explanation is grounded in the verified sequence.

### Expected result
The AI can still say:
- “there was a potential changeover reduction opportunity”

But if the sequence did not realize it, it should not say:
- “changeover reduction is the primary achieved strategy”

---

## 8. REQUIRE A FINAL CONSISTENCY CHECK BEFORE RETURNING EACH STRATEGY

Please add a final consistency check for each generated AI strategy.

### Goal
Before returning the strategy card, verify:
- does the final sequence actually reflect the claimed primary dimension?
- do the measured metrics support the explanation?
- does the actual result confirm or contradict the claimed emphasis?
- does the title overstate what happened?

### Expected result
If inconsistency is detected, revise:
- title
- summary
- 3D labels
- explanation text

so they match the actual sequence outcome.
