Please update the **order combination logic** so that compatible orders with different avail dates are still allowed to combine, using the **earliest date among the combined orders as the controlling date**.

This should apply to both **MTO** and **MTS** orders.

## 1. DO NOT BLOCK COMBINATION ONLY BECAUSE THE ORDERS HAVE DIFFERENT AVAIL DATES

Right now, it appears that otherwise compatible orders may be blocked from combining if their avail dates are different.

Please revisit this rule.

### Required behavior
If two orders are otherwise valid for combination:
- same material code
- same formula version
- within the 200 MT combine cap

then a difference in avail dates should **not automatically block** the combination.

### Goal
Allow compatible orders to combine even when their dates differ.

### Expected result
Different avail dates alone no longer prevent valid combinations.

---

## 2. USE THE EARLIEST DATE AS THE CONTROLLING DATE FOR THE COMBINED ORDER

When two or more compatible orders are combined and their dates differ, the combined order should inherit the **earliest date** among them.

### Example
If:
- Order A = Jun 10
- Order B = Jun 12

then the combined order should use:
- **Jun 10** as the controlling date

### Why
Using the earliest date is the safer logic:
- producing by Jun 10 also satisfies Jun 12
- producing by Jun 12 would delay the Jun 10 requirement

### Goal
Make combined-order date handling operationally correct and deadline-safe.

### Expected result
The combined order always uses the earliest date among the merged orders.

---

## 3. APPLY THIS SAME EARLIEST-DATE RULE TO MTS ORDERS

Please apply the same earliest-date logic to **MTS** combinations as well.

### Required behavior
If multiple MTS orders are combined:
- the earliest target/avail date among them becomes the controlling date

### Important
If the resulting combined order is still treated as an MTS-type combined order, then the AI may still suggest a date — but:
- it must not go beyond the controlling target date

### Goal
Keep MTS combinations flexible where allowed, but always anchored by the earliest target date.

### Expected result
Combined MTS orders are governed by the earliest target date among the component orders.

---

## 4. APPLY THIS TO MTO + MTS COMBINATIONS AS WELL

Please also support combinations involving:
- **MTO + MTS**

when they are otherwise combinable.

### Required behavior
If an MTO order and an MTS order are combined:
- determine which of the two has the **earliest controlling date**
- use that earliest date as the governing date for the combined order

### Goal
Let mixed MTO/MTS combinations use one consistent earliest-date rule.

### Expected result
MTO + MTS combinations are handled consistently instead of being blocked by date mismatch alone.

## 5. IF THE EARLIEST CONTROLLING DATE BELONGS TO AN MTO ORDER, TREAT THE COMBINED ORDER AS FIXED-DATE

If the earliest controlling date comes from an **MTO** order:

### Required behavior
- the combined order should behave as fixed to that MTO actual avail date
- it should not be subject to AI suggested-date movement
- it should respect the MTO-style date immutability behavior

### Goal
Preserve the hard-date nature of MTO when it is the controlling earliest order in the combination.

### Expected result
If the earliest date is an MTO date, the combined order is treated as fixed-date.
