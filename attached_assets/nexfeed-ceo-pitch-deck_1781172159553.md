## SLIDE 4 — How It Works

**Title:** How the AI enables smarter scheduling

**Section A — What the AI does:**

- Analyses the full production queue and suggests an optimised sequence to minimise changeover time while respecting delivery deadlines
- Presents two strategy options (Standard and Alternative) so planners can compare outcomes before committing
- Identifies orders that can be combined to reduce line changeovers
- Flags readiness gaps — missing formula versions, production order IDs, or Hand-Additive prep — before production starts

**Section B — Guardrails** *(build trust):*

- AI is decision-support, not fully automated — the planner always approves before anything is applied
- Built using actual Iligan plant data, changeover rules, and SME inputs
- Tested during pilot scenarios with production team involvement
- Designed with a defined scope: scheduling optimisation, not end-to-end autonomous control

**Speaker notes:**

Lean into the guardrails. The likely CEO concern is *"what if the AI gets it wrong?"* Answer it directly: the planner is always in control. The AI proposes; the human decides.

**Visual suggestion:** Show the Auto-Sequence modal with the two strategy cards side by side. Annotate: *"Planner sees both options and picks one."*
